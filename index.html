A New Model for Global Innovation

My journey began not in a boardroom, but in a lab. A PhD in Medical Biochemistry provided a foundational understanding of human health, but it was the distance from a lab bench to a patient's bedside that defined my mission: to bridge the vast gap between brilliant ideas and real-world, human impact.

This conviction led me to co-found SOUTH AFRICA MAKES, where we challenged the status quo of global healthcare supply chains. Using pioneering digital manufacturing, we developed a new model for local, context-specific innovation that now serves as a case study for emerging economies worldwide.

My operating principle is clear: 'innovation without inclusion is not progress.' This is the evidence-based perspective I bring to policy discussions at the African Union, G20, and BRICS forums, advocating for frameworks that ensure the Fourth Industrial Revolution empowers the underserved.
