A New Model for Global Innovation

My journey began not in a boardroom, but in a lab. A PhD in Medical Biochemistry provided a foundational understanding of human health—but it was the distance from a lab bench to a patient's bedside that defined my mission: bridging the gap between brilliant ideas and real-world impact.

This conviction led me to co-found South Africa Makes, where we re-imagined global healthcare supply chains through digital manufacturing. Our approach—local, agile, and inclusive—now serves as a model for emerging economies worldwide.

Today, as a Harvard Fellow and six-time founder, I continue shaping health innovation ecosystems that empower underserved communities while influencing policy at the African Union, G20, and BRICS forums.

My guiding principle is simple: 'Innovation without inclusion is not progress.'
